## 2024-04-06 - Replace Floating Point Math in Tight Loops
**Learning:** In tight loops like pixel processing (`preprocessCanvas` in `src/ocr.ts`), floating-point arithmetic combined with `Math.round()` is significantly slower than integer arithmetic combined with bitwise operators. For example, using `(77 * r + 150 * g + 29 * b) >> 8` instead of `Math.round(0.299 * r + 0.587 * g + 0.114 * b)` and `x | 0` instead of `Math.round(x)` cut the processing time by more than half. Precalculating values that don't change in the inner loop (like `y * width`) also yields a major boost.
**Action:** When working on pixel manipulation or other high-frequency calculations (like canvas data manipulation), default to integer arithmetic, bitwise truncation, and precomputing invariants outside the innermost loops to maximize performance in JS.
